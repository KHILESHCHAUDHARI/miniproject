Akola Tourism Guide

Explore the Cotton City of Maharashtra

Overview Things to Do When to Visit Precautions

Geography & Climate

  • Located in western Vidarbha, Akola has a semi-arid climate with hot summers and moderate winters.
  • The district is bordered by Amravati, Buldhana, Washim, and Hingoli districts.
  • Major rivers: Purna, Morna, and Katepurna

Tourism & Attractions

  • Narnala Fort – A historical fort built during the Bahmani and Mughal eras
  • Raj Rajeshwar Temple – A famous Shiva temple with religious significance
  • Balapur Fort – A well-preserved Mughal-era fort near the Purna River.
  • Katepurna Wildlife Sanctuary – A natural habitat for various species of birds and animals.

Economy & Agriculture

  • Akola is a major producer of cotton, pulses, and oilseeds, contributing to Maharashtra’s agricultural economy.
  • The city is known as the "Cotton City of India" due to its significant cotton production.
  • It has emerging industries related to textiles, food processing, and trade.

Connectivity & Transportation

  • Rail: Well-connected to Mumbai, Pune, Nagpur, and other major cities.
  • Road: Lies on National Highway 6 (NH-6),State-run buses and private transport services available.
  • Air: Akola Airport (Shioni Airport): Not operational for commercial flights, Nearest Major Airport: Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport, Nagpur (~250 km away).

Explore Historical Forts

  • Narnala Fort – A majestic fort located in the Satpura ranges, built during the Bahmani and Mughal periods. Offers stunning views and historical significance.
  • Balapur Fort – Situated near the Purna River, this fort showcases Mughal-era architecture and is a great spot for history enthusiasts

Visit Religious & Spiritual Sites

  • Raj Rajeshwar Temple – An ancient Shiva temple known for its divine atmosphere and religious significance.
  • Birla Mandir (Shri Ram Temple) – A beautifully built temple dedicated to Lord Ram, attracting devotees and tourists alike.
  • Shegaon (Nearby) – Home to Shri Gajanan Maharaj Temple, a major pilgrimage site just a short drive from Akola.

Try Local Food & Cuisine

  • Patodi & Bhakri – A must-try Maharashtrian delicacy.
  • Misal Pav – Famous spicy street food, widely available in local eateries
  • Shrikhand & Puran Poli – Traditional sweets worth tasting.

Wildlife & Nature Experiences

  • Katepurna Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Narnala Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Katepurna Dam & Reservoir

General Safety

  • Summers (March–June) are extremely hot, with temperatures reaching 45°C. Carry light cotton clothes, sunscreen, sunglasses, and stay hydrated.
  • Monsoons (July–September) can cause waterlogging in some areas. Check weather updates before traveling
  • Winters (November–February) are pleasant, but mornings and nights can be slightly chilly, so carry light woolens.

Health Precautions

  • Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid waterborne diseases.
  • Be cautious of street food hygiene; choose clean, well-reviewed places for local delicacies
  • Wear sunscreen

Cultural Etiquette

  • Dress modestly at temples
  • Ask before taking photos
  • Respect local customs

Wildlife & Outdoor Precautions

  • Do not feed or disturb animals—maintain a safe distance.
  • Wear trekking shoes and avoid visiting during heavy rains
  • Avoid venturing alone at night.

Travel & Transportation Safety

  • Drive cautiously as rural roads can have potholes.
  • Avoid night travel on highways due to limited lighting
  • Auto-rickshaws & buses are available, but confirm fares in advance.
